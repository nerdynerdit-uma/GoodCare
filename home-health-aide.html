Home Health Aide Services

At GoodCare Home Health Services, our Home Health Aide (HHA) services provide compassionate, hands-on assistance with daily personal care to help patients remain safe, comfortable, and independent in their own homes. These services support individuals recovering from illness, injury, surgery, or managing chronic conditions.

Our trained Home Health Aides work under the supervision of licensed healthcare professionals and are an essential part of a coordinated home health care plan throughout Southern California.

Personalized Assistance for Daily Living

Daily self-care tasks can become challenging due to limited mobility, weakness, or health conditions. Our Home Health Aides offer respectful, dignified support while encouraging independence whenever possible.

Care is tailored to each patient's needs and focuses on comfort, hygiene, safety, and overall well-being.

Home Health Aide Services May Include

Personal Care & Hygiene Support

  • Bathing and personal hygiene assistance
  • Grooming, hair care, and shaving
  • Nail, foot, and skin care (non-medical)
  • Oral and ear hygiene assistance
  • Deodorant and lotion application

Mobility & Positioning Assistance

  • Assistance with walking and ambulation
  • Help with transfers (bed, chair, wheelchair)
  • Repositioning in bed for comfort and safety
  • Changing bed linens, including for incontinent patients

Nutrition & Elimination Support

  • Assistance with feeding and hydration
  • Help with routine elimination needs, including:
  • • Routine catheter care
  • • Routine ostomy and colostomy care
  • • Enemas as directed

Basic Care Support

  • Simple dressing changes that do not require skilled nursing
  • Assistance with routine maintenance exercises or repetitive therapy tasks as directed by a therapist

Medication Support (Non-Skilled)

Home Health Aides may assist with medications that are self-administered and do not require nursing skills, including:

  • Handing medication containers to the patient
  • Opening medication bottles
  • Providing water or liquids to take medications
  • Reminding patients to take prescribed medications
  • Prefilling syringes when permitted by state regulations

Medication management and administration requiring clinical judgment are handled by licensed nursing staff.
